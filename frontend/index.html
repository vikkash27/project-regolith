MISSION BRIEFING

WHAT IS THIS?

Project Regolith is a real-time simulation of autonomous lunar rover swarms operating in permanently shadowed craters at the Moon's south pole. Each rover is powered by a separate Claude AI agent that makes independent decisions — no human in the loop.

WHY DOES IT MATTER?

Earth-to-Moon latency is 1.3 seconds one-way. A centralized mission controller is a single point of failure. This simulation demonstrates that swarms can self-organize, negotiate, and survive without ground control using economic task allocation.

HOW DO THE ROVERS THINK?

Each rover uses the Contract Net Protocol (Smith, 1980):

1Rover in danger broadcasts a Call for Proposals
2Other rovers calculate a utility score and submit Bids
3Best bidder wins the Contract and executes the task
4NVIDIA Nemotron audits every decision in real-time

U = (w₁ × battery) − (w₂ × distance) + (w₃ × capability × 100)

THE SHADOW THREAT

A lethal shadow boundary advances across the crater floor. Rovers caught in shadow lose battery at 5× the normal rate and suffer sensor degradation from thermal cycling. The swarm must explore, discover ice deposits, and evacuate before the shadow overtakes them.

RESEARCH VALUE

This platform enables researchers to study:

  • Multi-agent negotiation under time pressure
  • Decentralized vs. centralized swarm trade-offs
  • AI decision quality degradation under resource constraints
  • Reward model alignment (Nemotron auditing Claude)

Use the Research Panel (📊 button) during simulation to tune parameters live, view score trends, and export data for analysis.

SIMULATION PHYSICS

Semi-implicit Euler integration at 30 Hz. Lunar gravity 1.625 m/s². Regolith friction model (37° angle of repose). Battery drain 0.05%/s normal, 5× in shadow. Sensor damage 0.3%/s from thermal cycling in shadow.
